# NIST CSF 2.0 — My Notes

**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Source:** NIST CSF 2.0 Official Document

---

## What is NIST CSF?

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 is a set of guidelines that helps organizations manage and reduce cybersecurity risks. It provides a structured way to understand what needs to be protected, how to protect it, and how to respond to threats. It is flexible, meaning companies of any size can use it. Overall, it helps improve security posture and decision-making.

---

## The 6 Functions

### 1. Govern

* **What it means:**
  It focuses on setting rules, policies, and responsibilities for cybersecurity within an organization. It ensures leadership is involved and security is aligned with business goals.
* **Real example:**
  A company creates a cybersecurity policy and assigns a security team responsible for risk management.

---

### 2. Identify

* **What it means:**
  Understanding what assets (data, systems, people) the organization has and what risks they face.
* **Real example:**
  Listing all company laptops, servers, and sensitive customer data, and identifying potential threats to them.

---

### 3. Protect

* **What it means:**
  Implementing safeguards to secure systems and data from cyber threats.
* **Real example:**
  Using strong passwords, encryption, firewalls, and employee security training.

---

### 4. Detect

* **What it means:**
  Identifying cybersecurity events as quickly as possible.
* **Real example:**
  Using monitoring tools or SIEM systems to detect unusual login activity.

---

### 5. Respond

* **What it means:**
  Taking action after detecting a cybersecurity incident to minimize damage.
* **Real example:**
  Isolating an infected system and informing the security team after a malware attack.

---

### 6. Recover

* **What it means:**
  Restoring systems and operations after an incident and improving future resilience.
* **Real example:**
  Restoring data from backups and updating security controls after a ransomware attack.

---

## Key Takeaway Today

Cybersecurity is not just about tools—it’s about having a structured process from planning to recovery. The framework shows that security is continuous and involves the entire organization.

## SOC Analyst Relevance

| NIST Function | SOC Operation |
|---|---|
| Identify | Asset inventory — know what you are defending |
| Protect | Access controls, MFA, hardening |
| Detect | SIEM alerting, log analysis, anomaly detection |
| Respond | Incident response playbooks, escalation |
| Recover | Lessons learned, control improvements |
| Govern | Policies and compliance that frame all of the above |
